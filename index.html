1. RAVAS USA, LLC

What: Global leader in forklift-mounted weighing systems (iForks, iCP, iJack)

Size: ~200 employees | Netherlands HQ | $10-50M revenue

Why #1: CLOSEST match to your truck-weight-recognition pitch. Same technical problem. They capture weight/volume data from every forklift movement but do nothing intelligent with it. Tagline is "Creating Intelligence" but they have ZERO AI - purely load cells + basic integration software. Their "Weigh in Motion" tech weighs loads while forklifts move but requires manual SKU association.

AI gaps:

  • Computer vision: camera on mast to read pallet labels + auto-associate with weight data
  • Predictive analytics: weight anomaly detection for overloads, mispicks, theft
  • Load geometry analysis: visual center-of-gravity risk assessment for operator safety

    • Openers:

  • "Your Weigh in Motion tech captures incredible data, but I noticed it still depends on manual SKU association. What if the forklift could see what it's weighing?"
  • "You say 'Creating Intelligence' -- we can make that literal. We add AI vision to existing sensor data."

    • Red flags: Netherlands HQ = decisions may not be made at the booth.
